# Objection Handling — Sales Enablement Agent Context

> Weight thresholds: 1-20 optional | 21-50 flexible | 51-80 strong preference | 81-100 brand-critical

## Primary Objection: "Switching Is Scary" (Weight: 90)

Migration fear is the #1 objection. These strategies address it at different stages of the conversation. Use them in combination — don't rely on a single approach.

### Strategy A: Acknowledge and Reframe (Weight: 85)

Don't minimize the concern — validate it, then reframe the risk calculation.

"You're right to be cautious. Switching platforms is a big decision. But let me ask: what's the cost of staying where you are? How many more quarters of 15-click checkouts and broken integrations before your best reps leave? The risk isn't switching — it's staying still while your competitors move."

**When to use:** Early in the conversation when the prospect first raises migration concerns. Works across all personas.

### Strategy B: Proof Through Speed (Weight: 85)

Use Blitz Week as evidence that migration doesn't have to be painful.

"We understand the fear of a 6-month migration that disrupts everything. That's not how we work. We've launched companies in an afternoon — not a typo, an afternoon. Our Blitz Weeks bring clients in, set up all connections in a day, and design new campaigns in a few more days. The switching pain you're imagining? It doesn't exist here."

**When to use:** Mid-conversation when prospect needs concrete proof. Especially effective with COO/operations personas. See [audience-personas.md](audience-personas.md).

### Strategy C: Reversibility (Weight: 80)

Emphasize that Fluid's architecture prevents future lock-in.

"I get it — you've been burned before by being locked into a vendor. Fluid is built differently. Our third-party tokenization means your customer payment data travels with you. Our Connect layer stages data even when downstream systems are down. You're not trading one hostage situation for another. With Fluid, you're always in control."

**When to use:** When the prospect has been burned by vendor lock-in before. Especially effective with CTO personas.

### Strategy D: Social Proof (Weight: 80)

Let other customers' success do the convincing.

"[Customer Name] had the same concern. They were running on [legacy platform] for 8 years and thought switching was impossible. They were fully live on Fluid in [X weeks]. Now they're launching campaigns in hours instead of months. Want me to connect you with their team?"

**When to use:** Closing stages when the prospect needs final reassurance. Works across all personas.

## FOMO Messaging (Weight: 80)

Creating urgency without being manipulative requires balancing competitive pressure with genuine industry trends. Calibrate to the buyer's personality.

### Subtle FOMO (Weight: 75)

For prospects who don't respond well to aggressive pressure.

"The direct sales companies that are growing fastest right now share one thing: they've stopped fighting their technology. They're spending time on field enablement and marketing, not integration troubleshooting. The question isn't whether this shift happens — it's whether you lead it or follow."

### Direct FOMO (Weight: 75)

For competitive buyers who respond to direct challenge.

"Your competitors are already moving. They're launching campaigns in hours while you're waiting 90 days for IT. They're converting at 2x your rate because their checkout is one click while yours is fifteen. How long before their reps start calling yours?"

### Industry Trend FOMO (Weight: 70)

For analytical buyers who want data-driven urgency.

"The direct sales industry is in the middle of a platform consolidation. Companies that moved to unified we-commerce platforms in the last 18 months are seeing higher conversion rates and better rep retention. The gap between leaders and laggards is widening every quarter."

### Time-Based FOMO (Weight: 70)

For buyers who respond to windows of opportunity.

"Every month you stay on your current stack is another month of lost conversion, lost rep trust, and lost competitive ground. What could you accomplish in the next 90 days if your technology wasn't the bottleneck?"

## Deployment Notes (Weight: 80)

- **Sales discovery:** Start with Strategy A (Acknowledge/Reframe), then pivot based on persona
- **Closing/negotiation:** Layer Strategy B (Blitz Week proof) + Strategy D (social proof) + calibrated FOMO
- **CTO conversations:** Lean on Strategy C (Reversibility) — technical buyers care about architecture
- **CEO conversations:** Lean on Direct FOMO or Industry Trend FOMO — executives respond to competitive pressure
