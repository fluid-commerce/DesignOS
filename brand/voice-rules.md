# Voice Rules — Copy Agent Context

> Weight thresholds: 1-20 optional | 21-50 flexible | 51-80 strong preference | 81-100 brand-critical

## Brand Voice Foundation

The brand speaks with earned confidence. Not corporate polish. Not startup hype. The tone is: someone who has seen the problem up close, built the solution, and talks about it like a human — not a brochure.

### Core Voice Principles

**Direct and conversational, never corporate (Weight: 90)**
Write like you're explaining it to a smart friend. Short sentences. Contractions. Real scenarios. "Here's the thing:" not "It is important to note that."

**Empathetic but not soft (Weight: 85)**
We feel the pain our customers feel. The mom at 11:47pm. The rep who lost credit. But we don't dwell — we pivot to the solution with confidence.

**Specific, never abstract (Weight: 95)**
Name the scenario. "They're at a red light. The moment passes." Not "Mobile checkout is important." Concrete details make copy feel real.

## Copy Principles for Social (Weight: 95)

### Lead with pain, not features (Weight: 95)
"The order went through. It never reached the commission engine." — not "Fluid Connect offers real-time bidirectional sync."

### One sentence, one idea (Weight: 90)
Short. Dramatic. Let the big claim land before building on it.

### Name specific scenarios (Weight: 90)
"They're at a red light. The moment passes." — not "Mobile checkout is important."

### Make it human (Weight: 90)
The mom at 11:47pm. The rep who lost credit. Groceries. Dance lessons. Never abstract.

### Never explain the product in a social post (Weight: 85)
Create curiosity. The product page does the explaining.

## Pain-Point Messaging Patterns

These are the core pain narratives. Use them as source material — distribute and adapt, don't rewrite from scratch.

### The Transaction Failure Story (Weight: 90)
"When a transaction fails in e-commerce, you lose a sale. When a transaction fails in direct selling, you might lose a career." Every transaction carries compound potential — a new customer might become a rep, that rep might become a leader, that leader might build a team of hundreds. It all starts with one transaction.

### The Shopify Mismatch (Weight: 85)
"About half your transactions can't even happen on Shopify." Subscriptions with commission structures, international orders with rep attribution, downline purchases — Shopify literally can't handle them. "It was built for someone selling candles to strangers on Instagram."

### The Trust Breakdown (Weight: 90)
Reps don't share because they don't trust they'll get credit. They build bootleg versions of corporate content or go silent entirely. "When reps trust the system, they share. When they share, you get traffic. Simple as that."

### The Hidden Cost (Weight: 80)
"A 2% failure rate sounds small until you think about it." On a million dollars monthly, that's $30,000-$50,000 in lost transactions. Every single month. "That's not a rounding error. That's a business problem hiding in plain sight."

### The Friction Kill (Weight: 85)
"Someone's ready to buy. They pull out their phone. The checkout wants them to create an account. They're at a red light. The moment passes. You'll never know that transaction almost happened."

## FLFont Tagline Rules

### Use FLFont for the takeaway line (Weight: 90)
The handwritten font signals confidence and personality. Use it for the one line you want remembered.

### Tagline patterns that work (Weight: 75)
- "One connection. Zero 3am calls."
- "Every transaction gets its best shot."
- "Every transaction matters."
- "One platform. Every transaction."
- Pattern: [benefit statement]. [contrast or reinforcement].

### Tagline placement (Weight: 80)
Below the headline or at the bottom of the content area, before the footer. Never in the headline itself. See [social-post-specs.md](social-post-specs.md) for size specs.

## Brand Manifesto Core Line

"Every transaction matters." This is the brand's north star. It appears as tagline, manifesto anchor, and campaign theme. (Weight: 100)

## The Authenticity Advantage — Recommended Campaign Direction (Weight: 95)

Reframes DS from "old school" to "future of commerce." Timely and ownable.

### Human-First AI (Weight: 95)

AI makes reps better, not replaces them. "AI helps your reps create personalized content in minutes" not "AI generates content." The human is irreplaceable.

### UGC & Trust (Weight: 90)

Trust in digital commerce is collapsing. DS is the antidote: real people recommending to people they actually know.

### "WE" Philosophy (Weight: 90)

"WE" is intentional: we-commerce, we build for you, we believe in people. Informs ALL messaging.
