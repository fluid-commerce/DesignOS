# Droplets

## What It Is

Droplets are Fluid's extensibility layer — the open API and marketplace system that allows companies, partners, and developers to build new capabilities, integrations, and experiences directly inside the We-Commerce platform.

The simplest comparison: Droplets are to Fluid what the App Store is to Shopify. A growing marketplace of installable capabilities, built by Fluid and by third parties, that extend the platform without fragmenting it.

But Droplets are more than an integration catalog. They are the mechanism by which Fluid becomes infinitely extensible — and still coherent.

---

## How Droplets Work

A Droplet is a self-contained capability that plugs into the Fluid platform and behaves as if it were always part of it. When a Droplet is installed, it inherits the We-Commerce foundation automatically: attribution is respected, payments are trusted, sync remains intact, compliance is enforced. You are not bolting something onto Fluid from the outside. You are building within it.

This is the critical distinction between Droplets and traditional integrations. A typical third-party integration creates a separate data flow — its own system, its own rules, its own failure modes. A Droplet operates inside the Fluid ecosystem, with Fluid as the data owner and source of truth. A Droplet can store its own data, but it always interacts with the Fluid platform and admin to accomplish anything meaningful.

Installation is designed to be simple. For most Droplets, a company provides credentials or configuration details and the Droplet is active. No separate technical build required.

## The Droplet Marketplace

The Droplet Marketplace is where companies discover, evaluate, and install Droplets. It is available to all Fluid clients. In principle, any developer or company can access the marketplace by becoming a Fluid user — either as a client company or as a developer building for the ecosystem.

**Currently 20–30 Droplets are live in the marketplace**, including:

- **Avalara** — Tax calculation
- **Sovos** — Tax calculation
- **ShipStation** — Fulfillment
- **ShipHero** — Fulfillment
- **Extended Shipping Calculator** — Advanced shipping logic
- **Exigo, ByDesign, Pillars** — Back office Connect integrations (these are the foundational Droplets that power Fluid Connect)
- Data and reporting Droplets

The marketplace is growing. New Droplets are added regularly by Fluid and by third-party developers.

**Marketplace visibility** is configurable. A Droplet can be made available to all companies in the marketplace, or it can be built for a specific client and kept private. Functionally, private and public Droplets work identically — only the visibility differs.

## What Can Be Built with Droplets

**Custom widgets and interfaces** — New UI components, workflows, and experiences that live inside We-Commerce sites and apps.

**Third-party integrations** — CRMs, ERPs, fulfillment providers, analytics platforms, marketing tools — connected to Fluid without breaking attribution or sync.

**New commerce experiences** — Niche flows, vertical-specific tools, or entirely new ways to sell that are native to the We-Commerce cycle.

**Automations and intelligence layers** — Actions triggered by platform events: a purchase, a live stream engagement, a follow-up task, a subscription renewal.

**Partner ecosystems** — Agencies, developers, and technology partners can build reusable Droplets that are deployed across multiple client organizations.

---

## Droplets and Fluid Connect

Connect — Fluid's back office synchronization system — relies entirely on Droplets to function. The integration between a client's commission engine and Fluid is established through a back office Droplet (Exigo, ByDesign, Pillars, etc.). This makes Connect the one Fluid feature that is wholly dependent on the Droplet infrastructure.

From a marketing perspective, this doesn't need to be surfaced in detail. The key message is that connecting to a back office is a one-click process — provide credentials, hit install, and the sync is live.

## The Ecosystem Opportunity

The Droplet Marketplace is designed to grow in a way that compounds value over time. As more third parties build Droplets, more capabilities become available to every Fluid client. Companies get access to an expanding library of best-practice tools without waiting for Fluid to build each one.

This mirrors how the most successful platform ecosystems work — the platform provides the foundation, and a community of builders extends it in every direction. The capabilities of the Droplet Marketplace are theoretically limitless.

For Fluid, this also means that as the ecosystem grows, Fluid becomes increasingly central to the direct selling technology landscape — not because it has locked anyone in, but because it has made itself the most useful place to build.

---

## Why Droplets Matter

No platform can anticipate everything its customers will need. The companies that try to build everything themselves eventually become slow, fragmented, and unable to keep up with what the market needs.

Droplets are Fluid's answer to that problem. They allow the platform to grow without losing its integrity — so corporate teams get flexibility without chaos, reps get new capabilities without new tools, and developers get freedom without fragility.

The goal is not openness for its own sake. It is coherent extensibility — the ability to add anything, while the core keeps working.
