# FairShare

## What It Is

FairShare is Fluid's attribution system — built specifically for direct selling — that tracks the full customer journey and ensures credit goes to the reps who created the outcome. Not just whoever was last in the chain. Not just whoever owns the domain. But whoever actually showed up.

In direct selling, a purchase is rarely a single moment. It's the result of a shared link, a live demo, a follow-up message, a product recommendation three weeks ago. Traditional e-commerce only sees the last click. FairShare is built to honor the whole journey.

FairShare is not a feature companies turn on. It is the foundational belief the entire Fluid platform is built on: that when people's contributions are recognized, they keep contributing.

---

## How Attribution Works

FairShare tracks meaningful interactions — shared links, live streams, product views, media engagement, and follow-ups — and uses configurable rules to determine who receives credit when a purchase occurs. All attribution happens automatically at checkout, with no manual reconciliation required.

### The Four Attribution Settings

All four settings are live today and fully configurable per company.

**1. Shared Links**
When a rep shares a link and a customer purchases as a result, the company determines which rep receives credit. Options: First Touch (the rep who first introduced the customer), Last Touch (the rep whose link was most recently used), or Most Active (the rep with the highest interaction volume with that customer).

**2. Sponsored Ordering**
When a customer returns to the site and places a new order, FairShare identifies their sponsor from their previous order history and assigns credit accordingly. Companies can configure this behavior in three ways: honor the original sponsor, bypass the sponsor and credit a rep who shared a link to the customer, or replace the sponsor entirely so the sharing rep becomes the new sponsor.

**3. Orphan Orders**
When a customer arrives without any rep interaction or attribution — no shared link, no sponsor — the company decides what happens to that order. Options: leave the customer unattributed (orphaned), assign all orphan orders to one designated rep, or distribute orphan credit among the top-sharing reps in the company.

**4. Enrollment Credit**
When a new rep enrolls, the company decides who receives credit for that enrollment order: the new rep themselves, or their new sponsor.

**5. Manual Setting**
A fifth option allows companies to require customers to manually select a rep at checkout before completing their purchase. This is live but not commonly recommended — FairShare's automatic logic typically makes it unnecessary and removes friction from the customer experience.

## Order Journey

The Order Journey is FairShare's attribution audit trail — the official record of every interaction a customer had on their path to purchase.

The Order Journey displays every rep a customer came into contact with, every action taken, and the timestamps of each event. When attribution is disputed — when two reps both believe they deserve credit — the Order Journey is the source of truth. It removes the subjectivity from the conversation entirely.

Companies can view the Order Journey in the admin. It shows exactly what happened, in what order, and who should receive credit based on the company's configured rules.

## Media, Playlists, and Content Attribution

Everything shared through the Fluid platform carries FairShare attribution — not just product links.

**Media** — Videos, images, and promotional content can be shared by both reps and corporate. When a customer engages with shared media and proceeds to purchase, FairShare tracks that path and credits the rep who shared it.

**Playlists** — Both reps and corporate can curate playlists: collections of products, media, and content organized around a theme (a promotion, a product category, a seasonal campaign). When a playlist is shared, FairShare attribution is automatically attached. Any purchase that results from engagement with that playlist credits the rep who shared it.

**Any Page on the Website** — Through a global embed in the Builder, FairShare attribution is applied to every page of the company's website. This means a rep can share any page — the homepage, a product quiz, the "Shop All" page, a brand story page — and FairShare will track and credit any purchase that follows. Reps are not limited to sharing product-specific links.

---

## The Replicated Site

Every rep has access to a FairShare-powered replica of the full company website. Every page, every product, every piece of content on the company's site is automatically connected to that rep. When a customer visits through a rep's replicated site link, all activity is attributed to that rep — across sessions, across devices, and even if the customer returns days later through a Google search.

This is distinct from the MySite (see Rep Tools), which is a simpler, rep-curated storefront. The replicated site is the full company website, attributed to the rep.

## The SDK

FairShare includes an SDK (Software Development Kit) for enterprise scenarios where a company cannot migrate their entire web presence to Fluid but wants to implement FairShare attribution on their existing site. The SDK allows the FairShare attribution engine, cart, and chat widget to be embedded into a non-Fluid website.

This capability is live but is not a primary marketing focus. It is relevant for large enterprise clients evaluating partial adoption before a full platform transition.

---

## Payments and Attribution, Unified

FairShare is embedded directly into Fluid Payments. Attribution is resolved at the point of checkout — not after the fact, not through a separate reconciliation process. When a transaction completes, the correct rep is credited automatically, and that attribution data flows downstream into the commission engine via Fluid Connect.

## Why FairShare Exists

Direct selling only works when people believe their effort matters.

When attribution is unclear, trust erodes. Reps stop sharing corporate content because they've been burned — they sent someone to the website, someone else got credit, and they learned that the system doesn't work for them. So they stop using it.

FairShare exists to fix that permanently. Not by adding a tracking code reps have to remember, or a spreadsheet someone has to maintain, but by making accurate attribution the automatic default for everything.

When reps trust the system, they share. When they share, traffic grows. When traffic grows, conversions follow. The entire We-Commerce flywheel depends on reps believing that their contribution will be recognized — and FairShare is what makes that belief reasonable.
