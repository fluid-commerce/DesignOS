# The Problem We're Solving

## The Traffic Problem in Direct Selling

Traffic is the lifeblood of growth online. D2C brands never stop creating content — fresh UGC, new ads, quick stories, testimonials, FAQs — because once someone has seen a piece of content, it's stale to them. Freshness is the oxygen that keeps the flywheel alive.

Direct selling companies are stuck. Their reps aren't sharing — not because the products aren't good, but because the system has failed them enough times that they've stopped trusting it.

They sent someone to the corporate website. Someone else got credit. They built a workaround. The workaround broke. They stopped using the platform. Now they share their own screenshots and homemade links, or they stop sharing altogether.

And without reps in motion, traffic dies.

## The Trust Problem

The deeper issue is not technology. It's trust.

Reps have been burned. Attribution didn't work. Their effort wasn't recognized. They learned, rationally, that the risk of sharing corporate content wasn't worth taking. So they stopped.

When reps stop sharing, the company's marketing investment — every video, every product page, every campaign — goes unactivated. It sits in a Google Drive folder no one opens.

This is the problem Fluid was built to solve: not just making the tools better, but making the system trustworthy enough that reps believe using it is worth their time.

## The Shopify Gap

Roughly half of the transactions a direct selling company needs to process cannot be natively handled by Shopify.

Not because Shopify is bad software. Because it was built for a different problem. Shopify was designed for anonymous guest checkout — a stranger finds a product, clicks buy, and leaves. The platform has no concept of rep attribution, compensation logic, enrollment flows, or logged-in subscription commerce tied to a downline structure.

Direct selling companies attempting to run on Shopify are forced to stitch together plugins, custom code, and manual processes to approximate what they need. The result is fragility: broken attribution when an integration fails, missed commissions when a sync drops, subscription lapses when a payment error isn't recovered, and marketing content that reps don't trust to share because the link might not work.

The platform everyone says direct selling companies should use literally cannot handle the majority of what direct selling companies need to do.

## The Integration Tax

Most direct selling companies are not running on one platform. They are running on 10 to 15: a payment processor, a commission engine, a site builder, a mobile app tool, a CRM, a fulfillment provider, an email platform, a subscription management tool. Each with its own contract. Each with its own integration point. Each representing a new place for something to break.

The teams responsible for managing these integrations spend enormous time and energy keeping them alive — not building, not growing, not serving their distributors. Just maintaining the infrastructure.

Every integration failure has a cost beyond the direct operational impact. When a sync drops and commissions are wrong, reps lose faith in the system. When a transaction fails and no one knows why, confidence erodes. When a promo code doesn't work correctly because two systems have conflicting data, customers and reps both have a worse experience.

The integration tax is paid in time, in trust, and in distributor retention.

## The Rep Retention Problem

The best distributors in any direct selling company are the ones with options. And when the tools don't work — when attribution fails, when checkout is clunky, when the app feels like it was built for a different era — those distributors leave.

They don't always leave loudly. Often they just slow down. They stop recruiting. They stop sharing. They process their own orders and keep their checks, but they stop building. And slowly, the growth engine stalls.

Technology is not the only driver of rep retention. But technology that fails reps in visible ways — that loses their attribution, that makes checkout feel unreliable, that gives them no visibility into who is engaging with their content — actively accelerates attrition.

## What The Solution Looks Like

The solution is not a better plugin. It is a platform built from the ground up for how direct selling actually works.

One system where attribution is automatic and trustworthy. Where payments are optimized for the higher-risk classification of the industry. Where enrollment flows handle international complexity natively. Where subscription billing is flexible enough for any compensation structure. Where reps have real tools that respect how they work. Where corporate can build and launch without engineering bottlenecks. Where everything is connected — not stitched together.

That is what Fluid is.

Not a better version of what existed before. A different category entirely: We-Commerce — commerce built on relationships, where the technology finally reflects how direct selling actually works.
