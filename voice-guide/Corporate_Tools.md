# Corporate Tools

## What They Are

Corporate Tools are the administrative and management capabilities within the Fluid platform that allow corporate teams — marketing, operations, compliance, IT — to build, govern, and evolve the entire We-Commerce system without engineering bottlenecks.

In direct selling, scaling requires control. But control that slows the organization down defeats its purpose. Corporate Tools are built around a specific tension: giving corporate teams the ability to define standards, manage the system, and maintain brand integrity — without requiring that every change go through a developer or an agency.

The result is an organization that can move at the speed of the business.

---

## The Web Builder

Corporate teams use the Web Builder (also called Builder) to design and manage the public website — the primary digital presence where customers shop, where reps send their contacts, and where the brand lives.

The Builder is a drag-and-drop tool that does not require engineering support for day-to-day updates. Campaign pages, product launches, promotional banners, enrollment flow changes, and full site redesigns can all be executed by a non-technical marketing or operations team member.

Key capabilities:

- **Section library** — Pre-built, high-converting sections that can be dropped into any page
- **Multiple themes** — Maintain multiple site designs simultaneously, with one active at a time; switch the live theme without losing content
- **Version history and rollback** — All changes are versioned; previous states are restorable
- **FairShare integration** — Every page built in the Builder automatically carries FairShare attribution, so any page the corporate team creates can be shared by reps with attribution intact
- **International support** — Multi-language content and regional layout configurations
- **No developer required** — Marketing teams execute changes directly without filing tickets

## The App Builder and Mobile App Builder

Corporate teams also control the rep-facing app experience through two builders:

**Mobile App Builder** — Designs the mobile app experience for reps: what features are shown, how the interface is organized, what content is available by country, language, or rank.

**App Builder** — Designs the web portal version of the rep experience (the Rep Portal). The App Builder is the newer tool and is expected to eventually manage both web and mobile rep experiences from a single interface.

Both builders allow:

- Drag-and-drop layout customization
- Country, language, and rank-specific content variations
- Branded visual assets and color schemes
- Instant deployment — no app store approval delay

## Guardrails, Not Restrictions

Corporate Tools operate on a guardrails model rather than a restriction model. Corporate defines what is possible — the brand parameters, the approved content, the available features. Reps and field leaders work within those boundaries with meaningful freedom to personalize their experience and their content.

This is a meaningful distinction. Restrictions prevent action. Guardrails channel it. Corporate gets brand integrity without micromanagement. Reps get ownership without chaos.

## Admin and Order Management

Corporate teams have full visibility into and control over the commerce layer:

- **Order management** — Full order history, filtering, refund processing, and fulfillment management including split and held fulfillment
- **Subscription management** — Admin can take any action on subscriptions that a customer can take, including modifying billing dates, updating payment methods, pausing, and canceling
- **Enrollment management** — Enrollment forms, country-specific configurations, and pre-seeded international agreements are all managed in the admin
- **Promo code engine** — Create and manage promotional codes with full configurability by discount type, country, customer type, product, and timing
- **Order on Behalf Of** — Corporate and rep admin can place orders on behalf of customers and assign credit to the appropriate rep
- **Inventory** — Product availability management with bulk edit and warehouse support

---

## Sync and Data Management (Connect)

Corporate teams control the Fluid Connect configuration — the settings that determine how data flows between the Fluid platform and the company's commission engine. This includes sync scheduling, conflict resolution rules, and real-time status monitoring of all sync jobs.

---

## International Operations

Fluid's admin is built for global operations from day one. Adding a new country or region to the platform activates:

- Country-specific pricing (at the variant level)
- Market-appropriate payment methods (APMs configured per country)
- Localized enrollment forms and pre-seeded compliance agreements
- Language-specific content within the Builder
- Country-specific promo code and product availability settings

International expansion is a managed configuration process, not a rebuild.

---

## Why It Matters

Corporate teams in direct selling companies are often the bottleneck — not because they are slow, but because the tools they have require developer support for changes that should take minutes. Every product launch that requires an IT ticket. Every campaign that waits for an agency. Every region that needs a custom build.

Fluid's Corporate Tools are designed to remove those bottlenecks — not by eliminating oversight, but by giving the people closest to the business the ability to act on it directly.

When corporate can move quickly and confidently, the whole organization moves with it.
