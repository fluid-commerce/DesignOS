# Builder (Web Builder)

## What It Is

Builder — also referred to as Web Builder — is Fluid's visual website creation and management tool. It is where corporate teams control everything customers and reps see on the public-facing website: pages, layouts, product displays, campaign experiences, enrollment flows, and more.

**Naming note:** The correct product names are "Builder" or "Web Builder" depending on context. Do not use "Theme Editor" or "Theme Builder" — these terms underrepresent both the simplicity of the drag-and-drop interface and the depth of control available to more technical users.

Builder is not a standalone site tool. It is the rendering and presentation layer for the entire Fluid platform — the place where products, enrollments, pages, media, and commerce flows are styled and surfaced to the world. What products look like. How enrollment pages are structured. What customers see when they land from a rep's shared link.

---

## What Builder Is (and Isn't)

The **public website** is the central hub for customers and reps — where purchases happen, where the company is experienced, where links from FairShare lead. Builder is the tool corporate teams use to design, build, and update that website.

Builder is not the source of products, pricing, subscriptions, or attribution logic. Those live in other parts of the Fluid platform (Checkout, FairShare, Payments). Builder determines how those elements are displayed and experienced on the public site.

Think of it this way: Fluid manages what's true (products, prices, attribution). Builder manages how it looks and how customers interact with it.

## Key Capabilities

### Drag-and-Drop Editing
Corporate teams can build and modify pages without engineering support. Anyone who can use a standard productivity tool can use Builder. Changes are visible in real time and can be reviewed before publishing.

### Section Library
Builder includes a collection of pre-built, high-converting sections — product showcases, testimonials, call-to-action blocks, enrollment flows, FAQs, media displays — that can be dropped into any page and customized. This significantly reduces build time and ensures design quality for teams without dedicated design resources.

### Multiple Themes, One Active at a Time
Companies can maintain multiple themes within Builder simultaneously. Only one theme is "live" (published) at any point, but others can be built, previewed, and held in reserve. Switching which theme is active does not destroy the content or configuration of other themes. This makes seasonal redesigns, campaign launches, and A/B experimentation between full site experiences possible without risk.

### FairShare Attribution on Every Page
This is one of Builder's most important and underappreciated capabilities. FairShare is applied globally through Builder, meaning every page built in the web editor automatically carries FairShare attribution. A rep can share any page on the website — the homepage, a product quiz, the "Shop All" page, a brand storytelling page — and FairShare will track and credit any resulting purchase.

This means attribution is not limited to product pages or custom rep links. It extends to the entire web experience.

### Version History and Rollback
All changes in Builder are versioned. If an edit causes a problem, previous versions are accessible and restorable. There are multiple layers of protection in place to prevent unintended content loss.

### No Developer Required for Updates
Product launches, campaign pages, promotional banners, layout changes — all can be executed by a marketing or operations team member directly in Builder without filing a developer ticket. This eliminates one of the most common bottlenecks in direct selling marketing operations.

### Internationalization
Builder supports multi-language content and international layout configurations. Pages can be localized for different markets without duplicating the entire site structure.

### Performance
Builder-powered pages are built for fast load times globally, including in high-traffic markets where page speed has a direct impact on conversion.

## What Builder Connects To

Because Builder is the presentation layer for the full Fluid platform, everything built in it is already connected to:

- **FairShare** — every page, automatically, via global embed
- **Fluid Payments** — checkout flows built in Builder inherit the full payment infrastructure
- **Products and Checkout** — product pages, enrollment flows, and subscription sign-ups are built and styled in Builder using live platform data
- **Live Shopping** — live stream layouts and post-stream content pages are created and managed in Builder

This is different from a generic site builder that requires separate integrations for payments, attribution, or product data. In Builder, those connections exist by default.

---

## Corporate Control with Field Flexibility

Builder supports a guardrails model for the rep experience. Corporate defines what is allowed — the brand standards, the content parameters, the approved sections. Reps personalize within those boundaries. This keeps brand consistency high while giving reps meaningful ownership over how they present the company.

Corporate can build and evolve the experience without creating fragmentation. When the website is updated, every rep's attributed links and replicated site experience reflects the update automatically. No rep is left behind on an outdated version.

---

## Competitive Context

Most direct selling companies either rely on an agency or internal development team to manage website changes, or use a generic site builder that is disconnected from their commerce infrastructure. Both approaches create bottlenecks: either the marketing team cannot move quickly, or the site experience is disconnected from attribution, payments, and product logic.

Builder eliminates both problems. Corporate teams move at the speed of the business. And everything they build is already connected to the rest of the platform — no integration required.
