# What Is Fluid / We-Commerce

## The Core Problem

Most e-commerce platforms were built for anonymous commerce. A stranger finds a product through an ad, clicks a link, checks out as a guest, and leaves. The platform never needed to know who referred them, who should be compensated, or whether that customer was connected to a network of relationships that extended well beyond a single transaction.

Direct selling is the opposite of that. Every transaction in direct selling exists within a web of human relationships — distributors, customers, sponsors, downlines. Who gets credit matters. How the order flows through a compensation plan matters. Whether a rep's effort three weeks ago is recognized when a customer finally buys matters enormously.

Shopify doesn't know any of this. Neither does Stripe. Or Square. They were built for a different problem.

When direct selling companies try to run their businesses on platforms designed for anonymous commerce, the gaps are not minor inconveniences. Roughly half of the transactions a direct selling company needs to process — enrollments with compensation logic, logged-in subscription orders, downline purchases — cannot be natively handled by Shopify at all. The rest are handled imperfectly: broken attribution, missed subscriptions, failed international transactions, disconnected commission engines.

The result is a company spending its energy keeping fragile systems alive instead of growing its business.

## What Fluid Is

Fluid is the We-Commerce platform — the only complete commerce platform purpose-built for direct selling.

We-Commerce is the category Fluid has defined: commerce that is built on relationships, not transactions. Where brands and sellers win together. Where every piece of content can be shared by a real person with real attribution. Where a rep's effort is recognized, not lost.

Fluid is not Shopify plus a bunch of plugins. It is a unified system — commerce, payments, attribution, sync, web experience, rep tools, and extensibility — built from the ground up for how direct selling actually works.

## The Platform: Seven Integrated Features

### Fluid Connect
The data synchronization layer. Keeps a company's back-office commission engine (Exigo, ByDesign, Pillars, etc.) continuously aligned with the Fluid commerce platform. Bi-directional, automatic, real-time. One connection replaces a tangle of custom scripts and fragile exports.

### Fluid Payments
The payment infrastructure. Built for the higher-risk classification of direct selling, with multi-processor architecture, cascading payment retries, decline recovery, network tokens, and a full suite of alternative payment methods for global markets. Fluid Pay is the platform's native saved payment method. Every transaction is treated as if it matters — because in direct selling, it does.

### FairShare
The attribution system. Tracks the full customer journey — shared links, live streams, media engagement, follow-ups — and ensures credit goes to the reps who created the outcome. Five configurable attribution settings. An audit trail called the Order Journey. Applied automatically to every page of the website. The foundation of trust between reps and corporate.

### Checkout
The full commerce transaction layer. Products, subscriptions, enrollments, promo codes, order management — all built for the complexity of direct selling. Country-level variant pricing (not natively available in Shopify). Flexible subscription billing with independent billing, fulfillment, and volume frequencies. Enrollment flows with international pre-seeding. Order on Behalf Of for assisted-selling markets.

### Builder (Web Builder)
The visual website creation tool. Corporate teams build and manage the public website — pages, layouts, product displays, campaign experiences — without engineering dependency. Every page built in Builder automatically carries FairShare attribution, meaning reps can share any page on the site and credit will follow. Multiple themes can coexist, with one live at a time.

### App (Rep Tools)
The rep-facing business tool, available on mobile and desktop. Reps share content, manage contacts, follow up through Smart Links, and access Catchups — an AI-powered notification system that surfaces business opportunities proactively. Available in a standard version and a Co-founder (pro) tier with Unified Messaging, a personal business phone number, and advanced AI features. Corporate customizes the app experience through the App Builder and Mobile App Builder.

### Droplets
The extensibility layer. An open API system and marketplace — currently 20–30 Droplets available — that allows companies, partners, and developers to build new capabilities inside the Fluid platform. Tax tools (Avalara, Sovos), fulfillment (ShipStation, ShipHero), back office connections, and more. Droplets inherit the Fluid foundation automatically: attribution, payments, and sync remain intact. The Shopify App Store equivalent for direct selling, with theoretically unlimited extensibility.

## The Fluid Philosophy: Every Transaction Matters

In direct selling, a single transaction is never just a transaction.

A new customer might become a distributor. That distributor might become a leader. That leader might build a team of hundreds. It starts with one order — the first one that works.

When a transaction fails in this world, the cost is not a lost sale. It is a relationship that didn't start. A career that didn't launch. A rep who learned that the system doesn't work for them, and who will share less because of it.

Fluid was built around the belief that every transaction deserves to be treated as if it matters. Not most transactions. Not the easy ones. Every single one.

## The We-Commerce Flywheel

Direct selling companies have a built-in advantage that D2C brands spend millions trying to manufacture: a distribution channel of real people who genuinely believe in what they're selling.

But that advantage only works when reps trust the system enough to use it.

FairShare gives reps the confidence that their attribution will be recognized. Fluid Payments gives companies the confidence that transactions will go through. Builder gives corporate the ability to create content that reps can share immediately. Catchups gives reps the intelligence to follow up at exactly the right moment.

When those pieces work together, the flywheel spins:

Content gets shared → traffic grows → conversions happen → new customers create new content → more content gets shared.

Powered not by an ad budget. By people.
