Every Transaction Matters
When a transaction fails in e-commerce, you lose a sale. When a transaction fails in direct selling, you might lose a career.
Shopify doesn't understand this. Neither does Stripe. Or Square. Or any platform built for anonymous traffic and faceless checkout.
They built their systems for a world where customers are interchangeable. Where a 2% decline rate is "acceptable." Where a failed payment just means someone will try again tomorrow—or buy from a competitor. No big deal. There's always more traffic.
But you don't live in that world.
You live in a world where a single failed transaction might be a new mom's first order. The one she placed at 11:47pm while nursing her baby, finally ready to try the products her sister won't stop talking about. If that transaction fails—if the page times out, or her card declines, or she can't figure out who to contact—she's not coming back. And her sister, your rep, just lost the person who might have become her best customer.
That's not a 2% decline rate. That's a dream that died in checkout.

Here's what happens when you try to force direct selling through systems built for traditional e-commerce:
Payments decline at higher rates. Your industry is classified as "higher risk" by payment processors. That means your baseline acceptance rate is already lower than a typical D2C brand. A 3-4% decline rate that Shopify considers normal might be 6-8% for you. On a million dollars in monthly volume, that's an extra $30,000-$50,000 in failed transactions. Every month.
Friction kills conversions you never see. Someone pulls out their phone to buy. They don't have Apple Pay set up. The checkout wants them to create an account. They're at a traffic light. The moment passes. You'll never know that transaction almost happened.
Attribution breaks constantly. A customer clicks a rep's link, browses for two weeks, then comes back through Google. Who gets credit? In most systems: nobody, or the wrong person. Either way, a rep just learned their effort doesn't matter. They'll share less next time. The traffic engine slows down.
Content goes unshared. Your marketing team creates beautiful assets. Reps don't use them because they don't trust that sharing corporate content will benefit them. So they build bootleg versions—or worse, they stop sharing altogether.
Sync failures corrupt your data. The order went through on the website, but it didn't sync to the commission engine. Or it synced with the wrong sponsor. Now you're manually fixing records while reps lose faith in the system.
Landing pages leak conversions. Someone finally clicks through, ready to buy, and lands on a page that loads slowly, looks dated, and doesn't tell the product story. You lost them before they ever saw the checkout button.
Every one of these failures represents a transaction that should have happened. A customer who wanted to buy. A rep who deserved credit. A relationship that could have grown.
