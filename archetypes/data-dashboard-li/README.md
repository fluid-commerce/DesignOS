# data-dashboard-li

## What it is

LinkedIn landscape adaptation of the data-dashboard pattern: 4 stat columns spread across the full 1200px width, topped by a headline and eyebrow, anchored by a footer CTA.

## Structural pattern

The landscape format turns the Instagram 2x2 grid into a single horizontal row of 4 equal-width stat columns. A centered headline and eyebrow sit in the upper third. The four stats (value + label) fill the middle zone. A muted footer CTA occupies the bottom strip. The single-row stat layout maximizes readability at LinkedIn's wider aspect ratio — each number gets generous horizontal breathing room.

## Content type fit

- Multi-metric performance reports (4 KPIs in one view)
- Quarterly dashboards and annual reviews
- Competitive benchmark comparisons
- Before/after scenario comparisons across four dimensions

## When to use

- When you have exactly 4 KPIs that are equally important
- When the data IS the content — no narrative copy needed beyond the headline
- For results-focused LinkedIn posts that want a "dashboard" aesthetic
- When targeting an audience that responds to visual data density

## When NOT to use

- When you have only 2-3 stats (use hero-stat-li for 3, or minimal-statement-li for 1)
- When you need body copy or narrative context (the layout has no room for prose)
- When a photo is essential to the content (use split-photo-text-li)
- When stats need individual commentary or context lines

## Components

- Eyebrow label (centered, muted, small uppercase-style)
- Headline block (centered, bold, 44px)
- Stat item x4 (value + label, equal-width columns, centered)
- Footer CTA (centered, muted, bottom-anchored)
