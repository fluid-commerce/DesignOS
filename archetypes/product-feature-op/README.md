# product-feature-op

## What It Is

A one-pager product feature layout for US Letter (612x792) print and digital distribution. Leads with a value-proposition hero and presents four distinct product capabilities in a 2x2 grid.

## Structural Pattern

A hero section occupies the top third with a bold product headline and a brief supporting line. The middle section is a four-cell feature grid (2x2) where each cell carries a feature title and short description. A minimal footer with a CTA and URL anchors the bottom.

## Content Type Fit

- Product launch one-pagers and feature announcements
- Sales enablement leave-behinds highlighting key capabilities
- Platform overview documents for prospects and evaluators
- Event handouts summarizing product value

## When to Use

- The product has 4 clearly distinct features worth individual callouts
- The audience needs a quick scannable overview rather than a deep narrative
- Content will be printed, emailed as PDF, or shared in slide decks
- The story is feature-first rather than outcome-first

## When NOT to Use

- The story is driven by client results and metrics (use `case-study-op` instead)
- You have more than 4 features to highlight (split into multiple pages or use a different format)
- The product has complex visuals or screenshots that are central to understanding it
- The post is for a social feed rather than a standalone document

## Components

- Hero block: headline + supporting subtext
- Feature grid: 2x2 grid of feature cards, each with title and description
- Footer bar: CTA label left, URL right, separated by flex justify-between
