# hero-stat-li

## What it is

LinkedIn landscape adaptation of the hero-stat pattern: eyebrow label and headline occupy the left half of the frame, three stat values stack vertically on the right half.

## Structural pattern

The 1200x627 canvas is split into two vertical zones. The left zone (roughly 50%) holds a short eyebrow line, a bold headline in large type, and a muted supporting line anchored to the bottom of that zone. The right zone holds three stat items stacked vertically, each with a large numerical value and a short descriptor label. The horizontal split maximizes the visual contrast between narrative (left) and data (right).

## Content type fit

- Quarterly and annual performance summaries
- Product launch metrics (conversions, signups, time savings)
- Before/after comparisons expressed as multiple metrics
- Report teasers that drive clicks to full articles

## When to use

- When you have 3 KPIs that together tell a story
- When the headline provides context and the stats provide proof
- For LinkedIn thought leadership posts about company results
- When you want a data-first visual with editorial framing

## When NOT to use

- When you have only one dominant stat (use stat-hero-single instead)
- When the content is a testimonial or quote (use quote-testimonial-li)
- When you need a photo in the layout (use split-photo-text-li)
- When the post is primarily about a written idea, not data

## Components

- Eyebrow label (small caps-style label, muted color)
- Headline block (large bold type, left-aligned)
- Body copy (supporting line, muted color, bottom-anchored)
- Stat item x3 (value + label pair, right column)
