# case-study-op

## What It Is

A one-pager case study layout for US Letter (612x792) print and digital distribution. Presents a client transformation story through a hero headline, three impact stats, and a two-column challenge/solution narrative.

## Structural Pattern

Top hero zone anchors the story with a bold headline and 2-3 sentence summary. A full-width three-column stat strip immediately below quantifies the outcome. The main body divides into two equal columns — challenge on the left, solution on the right — giving equal visual weight to the problem and the fix. A minimal footer carries the CTA.

## Content Type Fit

- B2B case studies and customer success stories
- ROI or transformation reports
- Before/after narratives with measurable outcomes
- Sales enablement one-pagers for prospects

## When to Use

- The story has 2-3 quantifiable metrics worth calling out prominently
- The challenge and solution are roughly equal in length and importance
- The audience is a decision-maker who needs both the problem framing and the resolution
- Content needs to print cleanly on US Letter or be shared as a PDF

## When NOT to Use

- The story lacks measurable stats (use `company-overview-op` for qualitative narratives)
- Content requires more than one page (this archetype enforces single-page overflow:hidden)
- Visual imagery or product screenshots are central to the story (use a template with image slots)
- The post is for a social feed rather than a standalone document

## Components

- Stat strip: 3-column grid of value + label pairs
- Two-column body grid: challenge column / solution column
- Minimal footer: CTA label anchored to bottom via `margin-top: auto`
