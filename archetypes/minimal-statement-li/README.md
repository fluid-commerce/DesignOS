# minimal-statement-li

## What it is

LinkedIn landscape adaptation of the minimal-statement pattern: a single bold statement fills the wide canvas with generous horizontal breathing room and a supporting line below.

## Structural pattern

The entire 1200x627 canvas is treated as a typographic stage. Content is vertically centered with generous left/right margins (120px each side). A large headline (96px, 900 weight) dominates the composition. A muted supporting line (32px) sits below with a 24px gap. The landscape format gives the headline more horizontal space to run — where Instagram forces tight wrapping on the 1080px square, LinkedIn's wider canvas lets longer statements breathe as a single line.

## Content type fit

- Bold opinion or thesis statements
- Provocative question or challenge posts
- Single-idea thought leadership
- Hook-first posts designed to stop the scroll
- Motivational or mindset content

## When to use

- When the idea is powerful enough to stand alone
- When you want maximum typographic impact with minimal visual noise
- For thought leadership posts where the text IS the brand signal
- When brevity and confidence are the message

## When NOT to use

- When you have supporting data or statistics (use hero-stat-li or data-dashboard-li)
- When a photo adds essential context (use split-photo-text-li)
- When you're sharing a testimonial (use quote-testimonial-li)
- When the content requires more than 2 text zones

## Components

- Headline block (centered vertically, large bold type, full-width)
- Supporting line (secondary text, muted color, below headline)
