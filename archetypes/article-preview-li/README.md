# article-preview-li

## What it is

LinkedIn-native article card layout: category label at top, large article title, 2-3 line excerpt, and byline — mimicking the editorial article share card format native to LinkedIn content.

## Structural pattern

A clean editorial stack flows top-to-bottom. A small muted category label sits at the top-left. Below it, a large bold article title (72px, 900 weight) takes up most of the visual weight — wide enough on the 1200px canvas to run on 2-3 lines at most. Near the bottom, a muted excerpt (26px) provides a teaser hook. A byline row anchors the bottom-left with a small dot accent and the author name. The layout is intentionally text-only — no image zones — emphasizing editorial credibility.

## Content type fit

- LinkedIn article teaser posts (promoting a long-form article)
- Newsletter issue announcements
- Blog post or report previews
- Thought leadership content with a clear thesis
- "Read the full piece" link posts

## When to use

- When you're sharing or promoting an article, blog post, or newsletter
- When the title is strong enough to hook without a photo
- For professional/editorial content that benefits from a clean typographic treatment
- When author attribution adds credibility to the piece

## When NOT to use

- When you need to show data alongside the title (use hero-stat-li)
- When a photo is central to the story (use split-photo-text-li)
- When you want one bold statement with no editorial framing (use minimal-statement-li)
- When sharing a testimonial or quote from someone else (use quote-testimonial-li)

## Components

- Category label (muted, top-left, small letter-spaced)
- Article title (dominant, large bold type, full-width)
- Excerpt block (muted body copy, bottom-left anchor)
- Byline row (dot separator + author name, bottom strip)
